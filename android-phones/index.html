Cheap Android Phones in 2012

Cheap Android phones makes it possible for the low budget peoples to hold an amazing OS which was earlier seen only in high end smartphones. Recently Android powered devices are available in the market for price even less than $100. Now, in the beginning of 2012 we are witnessing a steady flow of cheap Android phones hitting the market. It is because of the open source nature of the Android OS that makes this possible. OEMs need not build their own OS for their handset, there's an already proven powerful open source software available for anyone to use. That's why we are seeing same OS powering high end devices like Galaxy Nexus or Razr Maxx, on the entry level devices. Continue reading →

Motorola Droid Razr MAXX arrives on January 26th

The much awaited phone Motorola Droid Maxx is finally gonna be released on January 26th.  Initially the device will be available for purchase only through Verizon Wireless Network's stores. The Razr Maxx is a larger version of the original Motorola Droid Razr. The main improvements are in the dimensions and battery. Motorola claims that it is the long lasting phone which is impossibly thin. The 3300 mAh battery is expected to power up the device for a straight 21 hours of talk time. Even if the device is released with Gingerbread OS, the official Ice cream Sandwich update will be released soon. With 8.99mm thickness, it is one of the thinnest smartphones ever released.

 

Features:

OS: Android 2.3.5 (Gingerbread) Android 4.0 (Ice Cream Sandwich) upgradeable

CAMERA: 8 MP (rear facing camera)

VIDEO CAPTURE: 1080p HD, tri-microphone for audio scene selection, 1920X1080 resolution, up to 30fps capture and playback

TALK TIME: Up to 21.5 hours of continuous talk time.

BATTERY TYPE: 3300 mAh Li Ion

STANDBY TIME: Up to 380 hrs.

DISPLAY SIZE: 4.3-in.; Super AMOLED Advanced qHD(540 x 960)

CPU: Dual-core 1.2 GHz Cortex-A9

Jan 17 2012

HTC HD2 Gets a Touch Capable ClockworkMod Recovery

HTC HD2- The most developed phone of all the time, gets another great modification. A senior developer in the XDA-Developer team released the touch enabled version of ClockworkMod Recovery. In CWR, which is installed on all devices, navigation was possible only through the use of volume up / down buttons. In this version, there are some touch buttons on the bottom of the screen for navigation and for making selections. It is currently in the beta version and the stable versions may be released soon.

You can view the original XDA-Developers thread for more information
